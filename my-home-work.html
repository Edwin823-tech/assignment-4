Travelling in Depression

When I was very little,I caught the travel bug. It started after my grandparents first brought me to their home in France and I have now been to twenty-nine different countries. Each has given me a unique learning experience. At five,I marveled at the Eiffe Tower in the City of Loghts. When I was eight, I stood in the heart of Poazza San Marco
feeding hordes of pigeons,then glided down venetian wateerways on sleek gondolas, At thirteen,I saw the ancient,
megalithic structure of stonehenge and walked along the great wall of China, amazed that the thousand-year-old
stone were still in place.
It was through exploring cultures around the world that I first became interested in language.

It begain with french, which taught me the importance of pronounciation.I remember once asking a store owner in Paris where Rue des Pyramides was.But when I pronounced it PYR-a-mides instead of pyr-A-mides, with more accent on the A,she loolked at me bewildered.In the eighth grade, I became fascinated with Spanish and aware of its similarities with English through cognates.Baseball in Spanish, for example,is beisbol, which looks different but sounds nearly the same. This was incredible as it made speech and comprehension more fluid, and even today I find the cognates come to the rescue when I forget how say something in Spanish. Then in High school, I developed an enthusiasm for Chinese. As I studied Chinese at my school, I marveled how if just one stroke was missing from a character, the meaning is lost. I loved how long words were combining simpler characters,so Huo () meaning fire and shan () meaning mountain can be joined to create Houshan (), which mans volcano. I love spenfing hours at a time practicing the characters and I can feel the beauty and rhythm as I form them.
